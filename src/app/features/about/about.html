About me

The story behind the code.

I’m Mariam, a curious software professional based in Tbilisi. I enjoy understanding how things work, solving practical problems and turning complicated ideas into clear, useful solutions.

How I got here

My path into software has not been limited to one technology or one type of role. I started in technical support, where I learned how to investigate problems, communicate clearly and stay calm when systems were not working as expected.

Later, I moved into banking products and payment systems. In my daily work, I use PL/SQL, investigate failed transactions, validate database scripts, work with APIs and help solve production-related issues. This experience taught me to care not only about how software looks, but also about how reliable it is.

I also had international frontend experience in Spain, where I worked on a route-optimisation feature and an interactive map. That experience made me more confident working in a new environment, collaborating across language differences and learning through real responsibility.

Today, I’m developing deeper skills in Angular, TypeScript and RxJS, while continuing to grow in databases, APIs and software engineering as a whole.

How I like to work

I ask why

I like understanding the reason behind a solution instead of only memorising what to write.

I learn by building

I understand concepts best when I can apply them, break something, investigate it and improve the result.

I care about reliability

A polished interface matters, but so do correct data, stable flows and software people can depend on.

I keep improving

I do not expect to know everything immediately. I value progress, consistency and becoming stronger step by step.

What keeps me curious

I’m especially interested in the space between frontend, data and real business systems. I enjoy learning how interfaces communicate with APIs, how data moves through an application and how software behaves in production.

I learn best by building something real, understanding why it failed and rebuilding it better.

Where I’m heading

I’m still building my path, but I know I want it to include meaningful software, strong teams, international experience and continuous learning.

I do not want to be defined by only one framework. I want to become a well-rounded software engineer who can understand both the interface people use and the systems working behind it.
